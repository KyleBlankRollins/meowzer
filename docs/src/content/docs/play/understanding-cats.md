## What Makes Meowzer Cats Special?

### üêæ They Move on Their Own

Cats wander around the page autonomously. You don't control where they go - they decide for themselves based on their personality and what's around them.

**What you'll see:**

- Cats walking to different spots
- Stopping to look around
- Changing direction when they hit boundaries
- Moving at different speeds

No two cats are exactly alike! Every cat has:

**Unique Appearance:**

- Different fur colors (orange, black, white, gray, etc.)
- Various patterns (stripes, spots, tuxedo markings)
- Different eye colors
- Various sizes

**Individual Personality:**

- Some are playful and energetic
- Others are lazy and prefer napping

Cats notice and react to things around them:

- **Food** - Cats get hungry and will approach food

- **Toys** - Playful cats love yarn balls

### üí§ They Have Different Moods

Watch a cat long enough and you'll see them change states:

- **Idle** - Standing still, looking around
- **Walking** - Moving to a destination
- **Sitting** - Taking a rest
- **Playing** - Interacting with toys
- **Eating** - Consuming food or water

## How Cats Behave

### Wandering üö∂

Cats love to explore! They'll walk around randomly, checking out different spots on the page.

**Why they wander:**

- Curiosity about their environment
- Looking for interesting things
- Just enjoying movement
- Personality-driven exploration

**What affects wandering:**

- **High energy cats** move more frequently
- **Curious cats** explore more areas
- **Independent cats** wander alone
- **Lazy cats** move less often

### Resting üò¥

Even digital cats need their beauty sleep! When cats get tired, they'll sit down or find a spot to rest.

**Signs a cat is resting:**

- Sitting still in one place
- Not responding to nearby items
- Moving less frequently
- Taking longer breaks between actions

**Energy levels:**

- Cats with low energy rest more
- After playing, cats may need a nap
- Some cats are naturally lazy
- Rest helps them recharge

### Playing üéæ

Playful cats get bursts of energy and love to interact with toys!

**Play behaviors:**

- Chasing yarn balls
- Pouncing on toys
- Following laser pointers
- Quick, energetic movements
- Excited reactions

**What triggers play:**

- Playful personality
- Nearby toys
- High energy levels
- Boredom after resting

### Eating & Drinking üçñ

Cats need to eat and drink to stay happy!

**Eating behaviors:**

- Approaching food when hungry
- Consuming food items
- Different reactions to basic vs. fancy food
- Drinking water for hydration

**Food preferences:**

- **Basic food** (kibble) - satisfying but simple
- **Fancy food** (wet food) - very satisfying and preferred
- **Water** - essential for hydration

## Understanding Behavior Patterns

### Why Cats Do What They Do

Cats make decisions based on several factors:

1. **Personality Traits**

   - Energy level affects movement frequency
   - Playfulness affects toy interaction
   - Curiosity affects exploration
   - Independence affects self-sufficiency

2. **Current State**

   - Idle cats may start wandering
   - Walking cats move to destinations
   - Sitting cats are resting
   - Playing cats interact with toys

3. **Environment**
   - Nearby food may attract hungry cats
   - Toys attract playful cats
   - Boundaries limit where they can go
   - Other cats may influence behavior

### Common Behaviors Explained

**Why is my cat just sitting there?**

- Probably resting or low energy
- May be a lazy personality
- Could be waiting for something interesting
- Just taking a break from activity

**Why did my cat ignore the food?**

- Might not be hungry yet
- May have low sociability
- Could be focused on something else
- Independent cats ignore items more

**Why is my cat walking in circles?**

- Exploring the area
- Hit a boundary and turned around
- Curious personality investigating
- Random wandering pattern

**Why did my cat suddenly run?**

- Burst of energy (playful cats do this)
- Chasing a laser pointer
- Excited about a toy
- High energy personality

## What Cats Notice

### Things That Attract Cats

**Food & Water:**

- Cats within a certain distance notice food
- Hungry cats approach faster
- Fancy food is more attractive
- Water is noticed when thirsty

**Toys:**

- Playful cats notice yarn quickly
- High curiosity cats investigate
- Energy level affects interest
- Distance matters - closer is better

**Laser Pointers:**

- Most cats can't resist!
- Immediate attention grabber
- Cats will chase actively
- Very engaging for playful cats

### Detection Range

Cats can "see" items within a certain distance:

- **Close items** (very near) - almost always noticed
- **Medium distance** - noticed if cat is paying attention
- **Far away** - only very curious cats notice

**Factors affecting detection:**

- Curiosity level (higher = better detection)
- Current activity (idle cats notice more)
- Personality type (curious cats see more)
- Item type (laser pointers are obvious!)

### Common Questions

**How long do cats stay active?**

- As long as the page is open!
- They pause when you switch tabs (depending on settings)
- No time limit on their behavior

**Can cats interact with each other?**

- Cats are aware of boundaries
- They avoid walking through each other
- But they don't directly interact socially yet

**Do cats remember things?**

- Cats persist their personality
- State carries over if saved
- But they don't "learn" from experience

**Can I control where a cat goes?**

- No! That's what makes them autonomous
- You can place items to attract them
- But the cat decides whether to interact
